# Track 6 — System-Level Topics

> **Where most curricula stop short.** Tracks 1–5 teach people to use AI. Track 6 teaches them to *change the work*.

## What this track covers

Four sub-topics, each with three levels:

1. **AI-Powered Decision Intelligence** — closing the gap between insight and action
2. **Human–AI Collaboration Workflow Design** — redesigning work, not bolting AI onto it
3. **AI Change Management & Organizational Transformation** — cultural readiness
4. **AI Value Measurement & ROI** — proving it was worth the money

You don't have to run all four. Pick the one(s) that match your audience's current bottleneck.

## Live session structure (60 min, applies to any topic at any level)

- 10 min: Concept recap — why this matters at the system level, not just the tool level.
- 35 min: Hands-on — apply the framework to one of the audience's real organizational challenges.
- 10 min: Implementation discussion — what's the next step in your org? Who else needs to be involved?
- 5 min: RAI checkpoint — what could go wrong with this initiative if RAI principles aren't embedded?

---

## Two unexpected lessons from running Track 6

1. **Track 6 is harder to staff than the engineering tracks.** Finance, PMO, and change-management partners are scarcer than engineers willing to demo a CLI. If you have one finance partner who'll co-facilitate Topic 4, build the schedule around their availability — not the other way around.

2. **Topic 2 (Human–AI collaboration) is the highest-impact session for non-technical audiences.** Several teams report Topic 2 was the moment AI adoption became real for them. If you have to pick one Track 6 session to run first, pick Topic 2 L100.

---

## Common pitfalls

1. **Running Track 6 before Tracks 1–3.** People who haven't used the tools can't redesign work around them. Sequence matters — at minimum, get the audience through Track 0 + Track 1 L100 first.
2. **Topic 4 (ROI) without baseline data.** "We saved time" without a baseline is unprovable. Topic 4 sessions should produce a measurement plan, not a value claim.
3. **Topic 3 (change management) as a one-off session.** Change is ongoing. Topic 3 needs a 90-day plan, not a 60-minute event.

---

## What success looks like

- 30-day metric: at least one workflow redesign per Topic 2 participant
- 90-day metric: a measurable value report per Topic 4 participant (even if the value is "we now have a baseline")
- Long-term: the org's AI strategy references this curriculum's frameworks in its planning docs
