# Adaptation — Customer Workshops (for sellers and SEs)

For customer-facing sessions run by sellers, Solution Engineers, or Customer Success Managers. Designed to be runnable by one facilitator with one customer team in 90 minutes to a half day.

---

## When to use this format

- A customer is **evaluating** Copilot, agent platforms, or Azure AI Foundry and wants hands-on context, not a slideware pitch
- A customer is **post-purchase** and needs help going from licenses to adoption
- A customer's executive has asked their team for an "AI strategy" and they want a structured starting point

---

## The 90-minute customer workshop format

| Minute | Activity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **0–10** | Opening — restate the customer's stated problem in their own words. No vendor pitch. | "You said your team spends 3 hours/week on X. Today we'll cover the track that addresses that." |
| **10–25** | Concept primer — pick **one track + one level** (usually Track 0 L100 or Track 1 L100). Walk through the concept and a public example. | 15 min max. Don't be tempted to cover two tracks. |
| **25–70** | Hands-on with **the customer's real scenario**. Facilitator runs the demo using the customer's own example (gathered in pre-call). Customer team participates live. | This is where the workshop earns its time. Treat anything else as warm-up. |
| **70–80** | Responsible AI checkpoint — apply the 3-question check to what was just built. | Non-negotiable. This is what differentiates you from a vendor pitch. |
| **80–90** | Scoped follow-up. Offer a 30-day check-in with one specific metric. | "What metric should we look at in 30 days to know this worked?" |

---

## The half-day customer workshop format (3.5 hours)

For customers who can commit a half day, run **two consecutive tracks** with a 15-minute break.

**Recommended pairs by customer maturity:**

| Customer state | Recommended pair |
|---|---|
| New to AI / pre-purchase | Track 0 L100 + Track 1 L100 |
| Has Copilot, evaluating expansion | Track 1 L200 + Track 3 L200 |
| Building agents | Track 2 L100 + Track 5 L100 |
| Post-purchase, struggling with adoption | Track 6 Topic 2 L100 + Track 6 Topic 4 L100 |
| Executive briefing | Track 0 L100 + Track 6 Topic 4 L100 (skip the deep hands-on; substitute strategy discussion) |

---

## Pre-call discovery (do this 1–2 weeks before)

A 30-minute pre-call with the customer's project lead. Goals:

1. **Surface one real workflow** the workshop will use as the hands-on scenario. Generic examples will sink the workshop.
2. **Confirm tooling access.** Do participants have Copilot licenses? Azure access? GitHub Copilot? If not, decide if you'll run it as a demo (they watch) or arrange trial access.
3. **Identify one success metric** the customer cares about. This becomes the 30-day check-in target.
4. **Set audience expectations.** L100 vs L200 vs L300 — pick one. If the audience is mixed, default to L100 with extension challenges for advanced participants.

---

## Pre-work for the customer

Send 7 days before:

- 2 Microsoft Learn modules from the chosen track (15–30 min total)
- 1 paragraph describing the workflow scenario
- 1 prep question: *"Where in this workflow do you currently spend the most time?"*
- The session agenda

If the customer is unlikely to do pre-work (often the case at exec level), shorten the live recap and lengthen the hands-on.

---

## What to bring

- **Backup demo recorded.** If live tooling fails, you keep going.
- **The customer's scenario worked through once already.** Don't run a workflow you haven't tried yourself.
- **The 3-question RAI checkpoint, written on a slide.** Not from memory.
- **A scoped follow-up offer.** Do not leave without offering a 30-day check-in.

---

## What not to do

- **Don't pitch the product mid-session.** This is enablement, not a demo. Trust builds faster when you focus on their learning. The customer will ask about pricing, packaging, and licensing on their own — answer those, but don't pivot the session.
- **Don't promise outcomes you can't measure.** "This will save 30% of your team's time" is a future audit problem. "Let's measure X in 30 days and find out" is a partnership.
- **Don't skip the RAI checkpoint** because the customer "isn't worried about that yet." They will be worried, later, in a less convenient meeting. Plant the discipline now.
- **Don't run more than two tracks in one session.** Even a half day. Cognitive fatigue is real.

---

## After the workshop

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| **+1 day** | Send recap email with: scenario worked through, RAI checkpoint findings, 30-day metric agreed, recommended next track. |
| **+7 days** | Light-touch check-in: "Did anyone try X this week?" |
| **+30 days** | The agreed metric review. Honest assessment — if it didn't work, debug it together. |
| **+90 days** | Adoption review. If the customer adopted the workflow, this is your reference story. Ask permission to anonymize and reuse. |

---

## What success looks like for a customer workshop

- **Short-term:** customer team can articulate the track concept and the RAI checkpoint a week later
- **30-day:** at least one team member used the technique in real work
- **90-day:** measurable workflow change tied to the workshop scenario
- **Long-term:** the customer asks you back for the next track. That's the loop you want.
